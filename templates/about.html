What is this all about?

I’m Mike Tigas and this is my eponymously-domained Web site. I write about work that I do, things that I see, trips that I go on — that sort of thing. I’d like to think that the blog’s title fits.

…What is it that you do, exactly?

I make Web sites for a living. {% comment %}I’m a Web developer at the Spokesman-Review, based in Spokane, Washington — though I’ve been known to pick up work on the side.{% endcomment %} I’m a freelance Web developer, primarily working for the news media and interactive entertainment industries. My expertise is in Web applications and backend programming (usually Python/Django), though I also have production-level experience working with iPhone application development and GIS/mapping toolkits.

Clients and brands I’ve worked with include: The Spokesman-Review (Spokane, Wash.), KOMU-8 TV (NBC affiliate; Columbia, Mo.), and Infinity Ward’s Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2. (There’s even more worth taking a gander at, on my portfolio.)

I shoot a lot of pictures, though I’m nowhere near as active as I used to be. I own a Canon 20D, but I prefer to use a Yashica GSN rangefinder loaded with Tri-X. (There’s nothing quite like the smell of D-76 and fixer, sticking to your hands after a good day’s work.) I spent a couple years as a journalism major and I’ve definitely had my fair share of fun with it.

I’m currently a student at the University of Missouri, on track for a degree in Information Technology, with a minor in Computer Science. (A couple credits shy of a minor in Journalism, too.) I’ll be unleashed into the world in May 2010.
